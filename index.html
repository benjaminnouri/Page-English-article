How to use Mount Stupid Concept for Leaders — Dunning-Kruger Effect and Imposter Syndrome?

Gazar
Published in Ehsan Gazar · 4 min read · May 19, 2022

One concept that helps you lead, is learning this, Mount Stupid. As a community leader, you need to know how to achieve wins with different people who have various levels behind people's reactions.

What can happen is when people start their journey with a completely unfamiliar concept, something strange can happen in the beginning from 0 to a certain level. It is a relatively new art area.

What is Mount Stupid?

To be understood it, you need to know Dunning Kruger Effect, which is a cognitive bias in which people wrongly overestimate their knowledge or ability in a specific area. This stems from a person's cognitive inability to recognize their lack of ability.

Key Points about Mount Stupid:

  • It represents the peak of overconfidence when someone has limited knowledge
  • Common in early stages of learning
  • Can affect decision making and leadership
  • Important to recognize and manage

In cognitive bias and psychology, sometimes we unconsciously try to hide certain traits we have that from these traits. Simply because it is higher placed in our minds.

The Valley of Despair

After Mount Stupid comes the Valley of Despair. This is where reality sets in and people realize how much they don't know. This can lead to:

  • Decreased confidence
  • Questioning abilities
  • Feeling overwhelmed
  • Potential imposter syndrome

Moving Past Imposter Syndrome

As leaders, understanding this journey helps us:

  1. Recognize where team members are in their learning journey
  2. Provide appropriate support and guidance
  3. Create a safe environment for learning and growth
  4. Build resilience in face of challenges
"The more you know, the more you realize you don't know." - Aristotle
